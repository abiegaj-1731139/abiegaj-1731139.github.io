Engineering Highlights of the S2000

The S2000 was incredibly light for a steel framed roadster, weighing in at 2750 lbs. It also had incredible torsional stiffness for the time. The car packed one of the slickest shifting 6 speed manual transmissions ever made, and had an incredibly quick steering ratio. Combined with responsive chassis and snappy rear end, the S2000 had one the most exciting driving experiences ever.

The 2.0 liter f20c engine was the most power dense naturally aspirated engine ever built. At 120 hp/L way up at 8500 rpm, it held this title until the $300,000 Ferrari 458 italia took it away in 2009.
